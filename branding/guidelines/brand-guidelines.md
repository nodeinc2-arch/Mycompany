# Node2 Brand Guidelines

Living document. Edit as the brand matures.

## Identity

- **Name:** Node2
- **Wordmark style:** "Node" + accent-colored "2". The "2" is the *node* — a deliberate visual pun (second node, the connection point).
- **Tagline (EN):** Building Canada's Next IT Layer.
- **Tagline (FR):** Construire la prochaine couche TI du Canada.

## Voice

- **Confident, not loud.** State capabilities plainly. No marketing fluff like "world-class," "synergistic," "best-in-class."
- **Specific over general.** "AI-integrated web platforms" beats "modern technology."
- **Canadian, plainly.** Lead with origin — say "Canadian IT company" early. Bilingual surfaces (EN/FR) are non-negotiable.
- **Technical without jargon.** Engineers and non-engineers both read these surfaces.

## Color

| Role | Hex | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Brand green | `#22c55e` | Accent only — the "2" in wordmark, call-to-action borders, hover states. Never as a large field. |
| Black | `#0a0a0a` | Primary dark background. |
| Off-white | `#ededed` | Primary text on dark; primary background on light surfaces. |
| Muted gray | `#a1a1aa` | Secondary text, captions, borders. |
| Rule gray | `#d4d4d8` | Light-mode dividers. |

## Typography

The site uses:

- **Sans (body & UI):** Inter
- **Sans (alt):** Geist Mono — for code, footnotes, "01/02/03" indices
- **Serif (display, italics only):** Playfair Display — used sparingly for `<em>` accents in headings, never in body

Avoid mixing more than two type families in a single document.

## Logo usage

- Minimum clear space around the logo: equal to the height of the "2" character on all sides.
- Don't recolor the wordmark outside the approved palette.
- Don't stretch, skew, or rotate.
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients, or outlines.
- On photographs: use the light-bg logo on dark/busy photos and the dark-bg logo on light photos. Verify legibility.

## Office identity

- **Toronto, Canada** — Remote office. Use for legal address, customer contact, time zone (ET).
- **Pune, India** — Development center. Registration pending. Mention as "development center" in external comms once formalized.

## What we are *not*

- Not a generic dev shop. We build IT *technology* — products, platforms, LLMs — not just websites.
- Not a foreign reseller. Engineering is in Canada (with India dev support); compliance is Canadian-first (CRA, PIPEDA).
- Not "AI-enabled" as a buzzword. AI is integrated into specific products, with named techniques (local LLMs, OLLAMA-class inference).
